At Fluxzero, your privacy matters. This page explains how we use cookies and similar technologies, what types we use, and how you can manage your choices.

---

## What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help us remember your preferences, understand how you use our services, and improve your experience.

We also use similar technologies (like session replay tools) to analyze usage and fix problems.

---

## Categories of Cookies

### 1. Essential Cookies
- **Purpose:** Enable core site functionality, such as security, login, and load balancing.
- **Examples:** Session IDs, CSRF tokens.
- **Expiry:** Typically expire when you close your browser, or within a few days.
- **Legal basis:** Legitimate interests / Contract (GDPR Art. 6(1)(b,f)).
- **Your choice:** Cannot be disabled, as they are required for the site to function.

---

### 2. Analytics & Session Replay Cookies

We use analytics tools to understand how our site is used and to improve performance and usability.

| **Provider**         | **Cookie Names (examples)** | **Purpose** | **Expiry** | **Legal Basis** |
| --------------------- | --------------------------- | ----------- | ---------- | --------------- |
| **Google Analytics** | _ga, _gid, _gat             | Tracks visitor interactions, such as pages visited and time on site. | Up to 24 months | Consent |
| **Microsoft Clarity**| _clck, _clsk, CLID, ANONCHK | Provides anonymized heatmaps and session recordings to identify usability issues. Sensitive input fields (e.g., passwords) are masked by default. | From session-only to 12 months | Consent |

## What We Don’t Use

- We do **not** use advertising, retargeting, or social media tracking cookies.

---

## Managing Your Choices

- **Via Cookie Banner / Settings:** You can update your preferences anytime.
- **Via Browser Settings:** Most browsers let you block or delete cookies. Check your browser’s help pages for details.
- **Withdrawal of Consent:** You can withdraw your consent anytime. This will not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.
